GCI- I didn't feel the vibe from it. I'd like to explain you a few points.

1) Being a proud gay has got nothing to do with equality. It is about self confidence of a person. When someone grows up, being different in feelings and emotions in a society which has no knowledge about it, the person goes through self struggle for years until he or she comes to terms with it and accept themselves. That's where the pride of sexuality comes in.

2) Different laws are needed because the community as a whole is still being suppressed and discriminated. Here in India, the situation is like the Law looks the face of society and society looks the face of Law when it comes to LGBTs. It is the job of the law to protect each and every citizen in a democratic country. We are asking for nothing more.

3) We have to show our visibility to get equality. It's good that your friends and everyone has accepted you but mostly people are not that lucky, neither the world revolves around you. Maybe for once you can step out of your shell and start putting yourself in the shoes of others who are not as fortunate as you.

4) How you stay with your would be or hypothetical partner or how you deal with your personal life along with your parents, is your personal setup, not of the LGBT community. Kindly do not mix the two frames.

5) Lastly, if the parental issue is concerned, keep their happiness aside. Are you willing to sacrifice the truth for the sake of your parents' happiness? I feel like there is no bigger a sin than lying. Now don't jump on me that I am also lying to my parents. Yes for now but when the time comes I will not think twice before coming out.
