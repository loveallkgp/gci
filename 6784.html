I'm Confessor #6770, and I've written a poem through the eyes of a Transwoman (which I am). It's imaginary, so if there's any resemblance that you see, it's a coincidence (and the condition in India).

I was walking into the woods the other day I could feel the trees moving to show me the way The birds were chirping and I could feel the cold air in my ears Suddenly, my eyes got wet and my cheeks were filled with tears

They took him away from me forever Saying that he'll just break my heart They didn't realize that what they were doing Was actually tearing me apart

My heart and soul are of a girl Sadly, my body is of a boy "Indian Society" makes fun of me Like I am some kind of toy

I've lied because I feared them I would rather die a true death than live a fake lie I believe the time has come to tell everyone I don't want to be a husband, I want to be a wife

I don't want to be a boy, I want to be a girl It doesn't even matter if it twists my world If I have to cry, I will cry If it gets me killed, I'm ready to die

I may be manly physically, but at heart I'm a female People don't go deep in my heart and think I'm a male I won't give this up easily without a fight Being the Gender "I" want to be is "MY" birthright

My heart has given up on my body too soon But my soul doesn't intend to give up on my heart I have always loved the feminine side of my sexuality And it hurts to be of the male gender in reality

I have talked to lot of people including my parents but they won't understand The way it's going I'm not sure if it will be a happy end

Whoever I am I want my parents to accept me that way Only then, after this ageing darkness, will be a beautiful day If I don't get accepted by my own, my life will be darker than the darkest night I don't want to do anything which will make me disappear forever from everyone's sight

I could've used a rope to hang myself by kicking the chair or the stool Or live my life like a boy repenting that I am the biggest fool I know it would be hard but whatever I am deep inside I want my parents to accept me that way If that happens only then, after this killer darkness, will be a shiny and beautiful day.
